Constitution of the Kingdom of Valoria

Preamble

We, the people of Valoria, proudly united under our Crown and operating as a sovereign realm within the Unite Union of Republics (UUR), establish this supreme framework. We dedicate our state to the generation of immense commercial wealth, absolute fiscal stability, fierce national pride, and the robust protection of our industrious citizenry. Valoria stands as a fortress of capitalist opportunity and a neutral, trade-focused intermediary, ensuring that corporate innovation and citizen well-being thrive hand-in-hand.

❦ ⚖ ❦

Article I — Realm Sovereignty and the UUR

  1. Valoria retains complete, non-negotiable domestic autonomy over its internal laws, judicial structures, provincial administrations, and economic policies.
  2. As a trade-focused middle power, Valoria actively cultivates global bilateral free trade agreements and wealthy mercantile alliances (such as our pact with the Astari), provided they do not conflict with active UUR treaties.
  3. In matters of collective defense, Valoria honors its exterior commitments to the UUR; however, no Valorian citizen-soldiers shall be deployed to foreign offensive operations without an absolute, explicit majority vote of the Valorian Regional Council (VRC) to ensure national blood is never spilled for external conflicts.
  4. Valoria maintains the absolute right to negotiate economic treaties independently to build capital and commercial depth.

Article II — Executive Governance & Power-Sharing

  1. Valoria retains broad autonomy over domestic affairs including law, economy, and local administration. Executive authority is shared proportionally to ensure elite consensus and decisive command via the Valorian Regional Council (VRC):
    • The King: Holds 50% of Council influence, serving as the immutable Head of State and supreme guardian of Valorian cultural heritage.
    • The Lord of Valoria: A fixed lordship holding 25% influence in the VRC.
    • The Senior Lord of Valoria: A fixed lordship holding 25% influence in the VRC.
  2. The VRC acts as the legislative body and drafts national laws. To ensure governance remains streamlined, it shall consist of these three core constitutional offices unless expanded by formal amendment.
  3. To guarantee fairness for all economic tiers, all drafted corporate laws, maritime codes, and tax rates must be published transparently 14 days prior to any council vote, preventing backroom oligarchic manipulation.
  4. True Democratic Pathway: Citizens retain the sacred right to petition and launch initiatives. If an initiative gains signatures from 10% of the active citizenry, it triggers automatic consideration, and a binding public referendum (popular vote) must be opened to the nation within 90 days, breaking any elite political gatekeeping.
  5. The Free City of Alter Hafen: Alter Hafen enjoys recognized, limited autonomy owing to its multi-national founding. It is governed locally by a fixed Lord (Lord of Alter Hafen) with powers delegated in municipal statute. Alter Hafen operates under Valorian sovereignty but retains local rule over municipal affairs and trade administration.

Article II.X — Lordships for New Cities

  1. When a new city is officially recognized by the King, a lordship may be established to oversee local administration, projects, and initiatives.
  2. Citizens of the Kingdom of Valoria shall participate in a one-time vote between the three current members of the Valorian Regional Council (VRC), to determine which official shall serve as Lord of that specific city.
  3. The elected Lord shall hold office over the city until reassignment, and shall be responsible for managing local administrative matters, reporting directly to the King.
  4. Lordship does not confer additional VRC influence, alter the fixed VRC shares, or affect the constitutional authority of the elected official at the national level.
  5. The VRC and King may enact regulations detailing the procedural, statutory, and administrative aspects of these local lordships.

Article III — Rights and Duties of Citizens

  1. All Valorian citizens are fundamentally equal before the law and enjoy baseline rights to safety, free vocal expression, private property, and a fair trial.
  2. As an elite merchant realm, civic participation and profitable employment are protected duties. The Crown guarantees that the free market remains competitive, unmonopolized, and open to any citizen possessing the drive to build wealth.
  3. To safeguard our borders against foreign overreach, citizens retain the right to bear arms. The state provides elite military and defensive training to all registered members of the territorial citizen militia.
  4. The government may take exceptional security measures in times of verified war or emergency subject to prompt VRC review.

Article IV — The Capitalist Engine & Strategic Neutrality

  1. Valoria's policy intensely prioritizes commerce, corporate innovation, and fair trade, seeking to be a regional trading hub and mediator. Regular taxation is legally capped at a minimal flat baseline to encourage maximum domestic reinvestment and reward financial success.
  2. To foster nationalist economic dominance, Valorian-owned businesses are granted strict preferential priority in all domestic government procurement and infrastructural contracts where appropriate.
  3. Valoria formally recognizes the legitimacy of negotiated free trade agreements (such as with the Astari). Trade agreements may be entered into by the VRC with proper council oversight.
  4. The realm maintains strict geopolitical neutrality in foreign wars, keeping our trade ports open and protecting corporate investments from international instability.

Article V — Security, Defense, and the Citizen Army

  1. The defense of Valoria is a shared responsibility. The Valorian Internal Service (VIS) maintains domestic order, while a trained citizen militia exists for territorial defense.
  2. Mobilization for external campaigns shall strictly follow VRC procedures. Where possible, Valoria emphasizes defensive readiness and avoids entanglement in foreign power struggles.
  3. The VIS coordinates with the UUR for joint defense obligations, subject always to the VRC's guidance and citizen safeguards.

Article VI — Culture, Identity, and Local Autonomy

  1. Valorian culture, language, and heritage shall be preserved and actively promoted by the state.
  2. Other cultures are respected within Valoria and in Alter Hafen's multicultural administration, provided they do not seek to undermine the Valorian constitutional order.
  3. Acts intended to provoke civil unrest through cultural mockery are legally discouraged and addressed within the bounds of law and proportionality.

Article VII — Treason, Loyalty, and Public Office

  1. Officials and citizens sworn into public office shall pledge loyalty to Valoria and respect Valorian national obligations.
  2. Treason, rebellion, or deliberate acts of subversion against Valoria are aggressively prosecutable under the law.
  3. The VRC holds the constitutional authority to review alleged breaches by high officials, while formal adjudication remains the remit of the independent courts.

Article VIII — The Rule of Law & Judicial Separation

  1. All citizens and officials are unconditionally bound by this Constitution.
  2. Laws may be enacted by the VRC and require formal assent as defined in national statute.
  3. To preserve logical fairness, the judiciary shall remain structurally independent from daily political interference, ensuring complete due process, proportionality, and transparency.

Article IX — Amendments and Citizen Referenda

  1. This Constitution may be amended by a two-thirds majority of the VRC and approval by the King, or by constitutional process initiated via citizen referendum when permitted by the VRC.
  2. For major structural changes (including alteration of VRC composition or core sovereignty clauses), a supermajority in the VRC and a citizen referendum are required.
  3. Procedures for citizen initiatives, thresholds, and referendum administration shall be detailed in implementing statute.

Article X — Implementation

  1. This Constitution takes precedence over ordinary statute. Transitional provisions shall be enacted to reflect the current VRC power distribution and the three-member VRC composition described herein.

Article XI — The Territory of Determa & Sovereign Integration

    Section 1 — Security and Strategic Protection

  1. Determa is officially recognized as a colonial territory under the sole sovereignty and protection of the Kingdom of Valoria, following formal approval from the UUR. The strategic military base left by the UUR in Determa shall be fully managed and controlled by the Valorian Crown to ensure regional security and defense.

    2. Section 2 — Local Administration and Governance

  2. The King of Valoria will manage the daily affairs, economy, and infrastructure of Determa himself, with support from the VRC. Valoria commits to establishing a stable, peaceful environment in Determa to prevent future revolutions, political unrest, or radical systemic changes. Valorian laws shall apply.

    3. Section 3 — Political Participation and Voting Rights

  3. The Valorian citizens of Determa shall have the right to vote in regular Valorian polls. Valoria continues the process of granting the people of Determa a "Valorianischer Pass". Only people with this citizenship or verified local property ownership will be allowed to vote in Valorian and Determian matters or actively engage in regional politics. To ensure fair representation, the "Determianischer Rat" is established. This assembly can draft local policies and suggestions, subject to final approval by the VRC and the King.

    4. Section 4 — Enlightened Corporate Expansion

  4. Trade between Valoria and Determa shall be protected and promoted. Valorian enterprises enjoy preferential trading and resource-extraction rights to rapidly build regional commerce. Companies are encouraged to expand into Determa. However, to ensure this is "good for the people," any mainland corporation operating in Determa is legally required to invest 5% of gross regional profits into building local infrastructure, modern housing, medical clinics, and schools, securing local loyalty to the Crown through tangible human prosperity.

The Valorian Law Codex

Title I — General Provisions

Article 1 — Purpose

The Valorian Law Codex ensures order, justice, and the systematic protection of Valoria's sovereignty, culture, economy, and citizens.

Article 2 — Supremacy of the Constitution

All laws derive their absolute authority from the Valorian Constitution. In any conflict, constitutional principles prevail.

Article 3 — Equality and Accountability

All Valorians are entirely equal before the law regardless of heritage, belief, or station. High office, elite lineage, or immense commercial wealth grants absolutely no immunity from criminal prosecution or anti-corruption laws. Code infractions by high officials are treated with double severity to maintain public trust.

Article 4 — Purpose of the Law

The Valorian legal system is designed to secure public order, absolute protection of property rights, business continuity, and the systemic rehabilitation of offenders back into productive, tax-paying components of the capitalist kingdom.

Title II — Crimes Against the Realm

Chapter 1 — Treason and Rebellion

Article 5.1 — High Treason

Deliberate actions intended to violently sabotage, subvert, or overthrow the Valorian Crown, its economy, or its collective alliances.

→ Mandatory Penalty: Complete asset forfeiture, stripping of citizenship, and permanent exile.
Article 5.2 — Sedition and Civil Disorder

Organizing, funding, or leading violent demonstrations intended to oppose lawful authority or promote widespread municipal disorder.

→ Mandatory Penalty: 3–15 days imprisonment or a heavy financial fine proportional to the economic damage caused.
Article 5.3 — Foreign Collaboration

Deliberate cooperation or sharing of secrets with hostile powers to Valoria's economic or military detriment.

→ Mandatory Penalty: 15–30 days high-security imprisonment and total asset seizure appropriate to the breach.

Chapter 2 — Symbolic Acts and Loyalty

Article 6.1 — Insulting the Realm

Publicly mocking Valoria's core institutions or national symbols in a malicious way that intentionally incites civil disorder.

→ Mandatory Penalty: Fines or civic corrective measures proportional to the harm inflicted.
Article 6.2 — Royal Disrespect

Unlawful, targeted slander or malicious harassment of the Royal Family intended to destabilize public faith in the state.

→ Mandatory Penalty: Strict administrative fines, mandatory community service, or public restorative measures.
Article 6.3 — Anti-Traditionalist Subversion

Publicly deploying banned, radical pre-reform symbols or organizing riots intended to intentionally mock Valorian cultural identity or incite internal race/class warfare.

→ Mandatory Penalty: Heavy financial restitution to affected neighborhoods or mandatory public corrective labor.

Title III — Civil and Social Order

Chapter 1 — Conduct and Respect

Article 7.1 — Disrespect Toward Authority

Willful refusal to comply with lawful civic orders from officials or targeted harassment of on-duty personnel.

→ Mandatory Penalty: Standardized fines or required civic service.
Article 7.2 — Threats and Harassment

Threatening, coercive, or highly predatory behavioral harassment against any citizen.

→ Mandatory Penalty: Heavy commercial fines or short-term confinement.
Article 7.3 — Destruction of Property

Intentional damage to public infrastructure or private citizen property.

→ Mandatory Penalty: Immediate 100% financial restitution plus additional punitive fines or community labor.

Chapter 2 — Theft, Fraud, and Deception

Article 8.1 — Theft

Taking the property, assets, or goods of another without clear and documented consent.

→ Mandatory Penalty: Full return of stolen goods, total financial restitution, or proportionate confinement.
Article 8.2 — Fraud

Deceit, corporate misrepresentation, or financial scamming for material gain.

→ Mandatory Penalty: 100% restitution to victims plus heavy court-enforced fines or confinement.
Article 8.3 — Exploitation of Loopholes

Exploiting obvious statutory gaps to gain an unfair, non-competitive market advantage. Such acts will be immediately remedied by judicial penalty and swift legislative correction.

Chapter 3 — Public Morality and Health

Article 9.1 — Drug Prohibition

Non-authorized production, trade, smuggling, or use of illegal narcotics. Licensed scientific or medical exceptions apply under strict regulation.

→ Mandatory Penalty: Custodial rehabilitation measures, asset confiscation, and trade banishment.
Article 9.2 — Disorderly Conduct

Public intoxication, violent outbursts, or indecent acts that explicitly disrupt the marketplace public peace.

→ Mandatory Penalty: Fines or forced civic service hours.

Title IV — Economic and Administrative Laws

Article 10 — Anti-Oligarchic Market Manipulation

Valorian and allied enterprises are given absolute preference in procurement to ensure national dominance. However, sellers, syndicates, or wealthy cartels are strictly banned from enacting "Phantom Bidding" or deploying fake identities to artificially inflate prices. Monopolistic market hoarding that suffocates small business competitors or deliberate sabotage of domestic infrastructure is aggressively penalized.

→ Mandatory Penalty: Immediate revocation of merchant trading licenses and a fine equal to 200% of the fraudulent transaction value.
Article 11 — Corruption and Abuse of Office

Public officials engaging in bribery, extortion, or corporate favoritism face immediate dismissal, permanent blacklisting, asset seizure, and severe criminal sanction.

Article 12 — Tax and Contribution

Valoria imposes a minimal flat regular taxation structure to protect private wealth. In exceptional emergency circumstances, mandatory civic contributions or defensive service hours may be temporarily enacted under direct VRC law and oversight.

Title V — Rights and Safeguards

Article 13 — Right to Fair Trial

Every accused individual has the absolute right to a legal defense, professional representation, and a structured appeal process.

Article 14 — Privacy and Dignity

Private correspondence, homes, corporate offices, and personal property are completely protected from unlawful intrusion or warrantless search.

Article 15 — Emergency Suspension of Rights

In times of certified war or critical national emergency, certain civic rights may be temporarily limited by lawful decree, subject to immediate, prompt VRC review and independent judicial oversight.

Title VI — Implementation and Interpretation

Article 16 — Judiciary Authority

The Royal Court of Valoria interprets this Codex, and its rulings set clear, binding national legal precedents.

Article 17 — Enforcement

The Valorian Internal Service (VIS) enforces this Codex under strict oversight of the Ministry of Justice and Security and remains entirely subject to judicial review to prevent police overreach.

Article 18 — Amendments to the Codex

Laws within this Codex may be modified by the VRC with comprehensive council oversight and the formal assent required by the Constitution.

Title VII — Special Provisions

Article 19 — Alter Hafen Autonomy

Alter Hafen retains its historic municipal autonomy in local governance, trade administration, and cultural affairs consistent with Valorian law. Disputes between Alter Hafen officials and Valorian authorities are resolved through designated independent judicial arbitration mechanisms.

Article 20 — Trade and External Agreements

Valoria may enter trade and alliance agreements (including free trade with the Astari). Such agreements shall be structured to preserve Valoria's neutrality posture where possible and to respect core UUR obligations.

Title VIII — The Professionalized Court System

Article 21 & 22 — Strict Separation of the Judiciary

To guarantee complete logical fairness, daily civil and criminal trials are overseen exclusively by professional, independent Law Speakers (Judges) who are insulated from active political office.

The Supreme Constitutional Appellate Bench: The King and the two VRC Lords do not hear daily trials. They sit exclusively as a supreme three-judge review panel reserved only for high-tier corporate disputes, international treaties, or cases where a citizen challenges the constitutionality of a VRC edict. Every trial, piece of evidence, and final verdict must be logged privately unless exceptions are made, preventing leaking anything private from the subject. Final verdicts on this supreme bench require a majority vote of the panel. No secret evidence may ever be used to convict a citizen.

Title IX — Marketplace, Trading, and Bidding Laws

Chapter 1 — General Trade Rules

Article 1 — The Open Market Rule

All trades made in public markets must use official, standardized Valorian weights and measures to protect buyers and prevent fraud.

Article 2 — The Verbal Bond

A verbal agreement to trade goods is legally binding if witnessed by at least one neutral citizen or recorded in writing.

Chapter 2 — Bidding and Auction Laws

Article 9 — The Valid Bid

Once a citizen places a bid in an official auction, they cannot withdraw it unless outbid by another party.

Article 10 — Phantom Bidding Ban

Sellers may not hire friends or use fake names to place false bids to drive up the price of their own items.

Article 11 — The Hammer Rule

An auction item belongs to the highest bidder the moment the auctioneer's hammer falls or the official timer ends.

Article 12 — Non-Payment Penalty

If a winning bidder fails to pay for the item within 72 hours, the sale is cancelled, and the winner must pay a fine equal to 30% of their bid. Sellers may sue for further damages in court if necessary.

Article 13 — Minimum Reserves

Sellers must state before the bidding starts if there is a minimum hidden price (reserve) the item must reach to be sold.

Article 14 — Auctioneer Neutrality

The person running the auction cannot bid on the items they are actively selling.

Title X — Civil, Property, and Daily Kingdom Laws

Chapter 1 — Land and Housing

Article 15 — Land Boundaries

Boundaries are made by the Crown or local Lords to define property lines. Moving a boundary marker secretly is a major criminal offense.

Article 16 — Abandoned Property

If a house or plot of land is left unused and unpaid for more than 60 days without prior notice, the Crown may reclaim it.

Article 17 — Right of Way

Citizens may cross private land if it is the only accessible route to a public road, well, or harbor, provided they cause no damage.

Article 18 — Renting Fairness

To preserve social peace and protect working families from predatory exploitation, landlords must give tenants a minimum of 30 days' formal written notice before an eviction can take place. Instantaneous evictions are strictly illegal, unless the tenant has committed verifiable property destruction or has willfully refused rental payment for over 60 consecutive days.

Article 19 — Collateral Protection

If a citizen borrows money using their home or tools as collateral, the lender cannot seize the property without an explicit Royal Court order.

Article 20 — Building Safety

Any structure built in a city must match the architectural style and structural baseline safety regulations of the district.

Chapter 2 — Contracts, Debts, and Labor

Article 21 — The Written Word

Partnerships or business agreements involving shared profits must be formally written down and signed by both parties.

Article 22 — Human-Dignity Restorative Service

Raw debt slavery and the physical ownership of individuals are permanently outlawed. If a citizen falls into a certified, unpayable civil debt, the independent courts shall place them into Restorative Commercial Service. The debtor is matched with a fair-wage enterprise, keeping a protected portion of their earnings for housing and food, while the remaining surplus is paid directly to the lender until the ledger is entirely cleared. This preserves the rich man's capital while protecting the poor man's human dignity.

Article 23 — Fair Wages

Workers must be paid exactly the amount agreed upon in their contract; wage theft by employers is heavily penalized.

Article 24 — Mandatory Industrial Safety and Corporate Liability

While workers must remain personally alert and undergo mandatory safety training, all industrial employers and wealthy business enterprises are legally mandated to maintain rigorously certified, structurally sound, and secure operational environments. If a worker is injured due to a corporation's failure to maintain safe equipment or updated protocols, the employer is automatically held fully liable under the law. The enterprise must fund 100% of the worker's medical recovery and provide full wage compensation during rehabilitation.

Article 25 — Money Oversight

The VRC or King can request to see salary and company fiscal records to prevent corporate tax evasion or labor exploitation.

Article 26 — Contract Breaking

Walking away from a half-finished building or service contract without a valid emergency allows the victim to claim full damages in court.

Chapter 3 — Harbor Rules

Article 27 — Smuggling

Bringing goods into Valoria without passing through official harbor checkpoints to avoid trade duties will result in the immediate confiscation of the cargo and commercial fines.

Title XI — Public Order, Safety, and Military Laws

Chapter 1 — Weaponry and Military Service

Article 28 — Weapon Registration

While people have the right to bear arms for defense, firearms require a specific license from the VRC to track distribution.

Article 29 — Conscription Readiness

In times of defensive emergency, every able-bodied citizen registered in the militia must report to their local post within 48 hours of the horn blast.

Article 30 — Militia Care

Citizens must keep their personal armor and weapons clean, functional, and ready for inspection by the VIS.

Article 31 — Banned Public Drawing

Drawing a weapon in an aggressive manner inside a tavern or public square, unless in clear self-defense, is a severe breach of peace.

Article 32 — Desertion

Running away from the militia line during a defensive battle is punishable by immediate exile and total loss of property.

Chapter 2 — Public Peace and Neighborhood Rules

Article 33 — Night Curfew Lights

To prevent localized theft and arson, all merchant storefronts must maintain standard illumination or follow local evening district rules as set by the municipal Lords.

Chapter 3 — Public Goods and Infrastructure

Article 34 — Road Maintenance

Citizens are forbidden from digging up, destroying, or blocking public roads without explicit permission from the local district Lord.

Article 35 — Vandalism of Signs

Defacing or destroying official Crown notices, bounty boards, or street signs will result in mandatory cleanup duties and restitution fines.

Article 36 — Spying and Espionage

Passing internal VRC documents, economic secrets, or military defense plans to foreign states is treated as Foreign Collaboration.

Article 37 — Diplomatic Immunity

Official ambassadors sent by allies (such as the Astari) cannot be arrested by the VIS without the King's personal approval, unless they pose an immediate physical threat.

Article 38 — Bilateral Treaty Violations

Any Valorian merchant who intentionally breaks a terms-of-trade agreement signed between Valoria and an allied nation can have their trading license permanently revoked by the Court.

Closing Clause

Let this Enlightened Codex stand as the ultimate shield of our realm. It guarantees that Valoria remains an unmatched heaven of elite capitalist accumulation, a powerhouse of nationalist pride, and a safe, legally logical fortress of stability for every worker who drives our nation forward.
