Constitution of the Kingdom of Valoria

Preamble

We, the people of Valoria, united under our King and loyal within the Unite Union of Republics (UUR), establish this Constitution to preserve our sovereignty, ensure justice and security, and uphold the Valorian spirit of trade, neutrality, and civic participation. Valoria seeks to be a stable middle power — focused on commerce, alliances, and pragmatic diplomacy while remaining loyal to the UUR when collective defense is required.

❦

Article I — Valoria and the UUR

  1. Valoria is a proud and semi-autonomous realm within the Unite Union of Republics (UUR).
  2. Valoria remains a member of the UUR. Valoria's default posture is to act as a neutral, trade-focused intermediary ("the middle") that cultivates alliances and commerce with multiple partners, provided such relations do not conflict with UUR obligations.
  3. In matters of UUR collective defense, Valoria will ordinarily honor its commitments to the UUR; however the internal governance mechanisms (see Article II and Article V) provide for considered decision-making before committing Valorian forces to offensive operations outside its borders.
  4. Valoria maintains the right to negotiate bilateral trade and alliance agreements (for example, free trade arrangements with the Astari) so long as they do not violate explicit UUR treaties to which Valoria is party.
❦

Article II — Autonomy, Power-sharing, and Governance

  1. Valoria retains broad autonomy over domestic affairs including law, economy, and local administration, consistent with UUR fundamental principles.
  2. Valoria's executive authority is shared among three constitutional offices that together form the Valorian Regional Council (VRC):
    1. The King — holds the decisive interest equivalent to 50% of the Council influence and acts as Head of State and guardian of Valorian unity.
    2. The Lord of Valoria — a fixed lordship holding 25% influence in the VRC
    3. The Senior Lord of Valoria — a fixed lordship holding 25% influence in the VRC
  3. The VRC votes on major constitutional matters and on whether a citizen referendum (popular vote) should be opened for issues of national importance. A majority of the Council is required for most decisions; certain constitutional questions may require a supermajority (see Article XI).
  4. The Valorian Regional Council (VRC) is also the legislative body. To reflect Valoria's current size and practice, the VRC shall be constituted of three (3) appointed/elected members unless otherwise expanded by constitutional amendment. The VRC deliberates and drafts legislation, subject to VRC and Crown approval as defined herein.
  5. Citizen political participation: citizens retain the right to petition, propose initiatives, and request referenda. An initiative signed by a threshold proportion of citizens (set by law) triggers VRC consideration and requires the VRC to vote on whether to allow a public referendum. This provides a formal pathway for citizens to influence policy beyond passive observation.
  6. Alter Hafen: the city of Alter Hafen enjoys recognized, limited autonomy owing to its multi-national founding. It is governed locally by a fixed Lord (Lord of Alter Hafen) with powers delegated in municipal statute. Alter Hafen operates under Valorian sovereignty but retains local rule over municipal affairs and trade administration.
❦

Article II.X — Lordships for New Cities

  1. When a new city is officially recognized by the King, a lordship may be established to oversee local administration, projects, and initiatives.
  2. Citizens of the Kingdom of Valoria shall participate in a one-time vote between three current members of the Valorian Regional Council (VRC), to determine which official shall serve as Lord of the city.
  3. The elected Lord shall hold office over the city until reassignment, and shall be responsible for managing local administrative matters, reporting to the King as necessary.
  4. Lordship does not confer additional VRC influence, alter the fixed VRC shares, or affect the constitutional authority of the elected official at the national level.
  5. The VRC and King may enact regulations detailing the procedural, statutory, and administrative aspects of the lordships.
❦

Article III — Rights and Duties of Citizens

  1. All citizens are equal before the law and enjoy fundamental rights to safety, freedom of expression, property, and fair trial.
  2. Employment, civic contribution, and participation are encouraged as duties of citizenship; the State shall provide avenues for meaningful, proportionate service.
  3. Citizens have the right to bear arms for defense of the realm subject to regulation and training requirements set by law.
  4. The government may take exceptional measures in times of war or national emergency in accordance with Article XV (emergency review).
❦

Article IV — Economy, Trade, and Neutrality

  1. Valoria's policy prioritizes commerce, innovation, and fair trade. The realm seeks to be a regional trading hub and mediator.
  2. Valorian and allied enterprises are given preference in domestic procurement where appropriate; however treaty obligations and fair competition rules shall be respected.
  3. Valoria formally recognizes the legitimacy of negotiated free trade agreements (such as with the Astari). Trade agreements may be entered into by the VRC with VRC oversight to ensure consistency with UUR commitments.
  4. Neutrality policy: Valoria will avoid participation in offensive conflicts where possible, seeking to remain the neutral middle. If UUR collective defense compels participation, the VRC shall guide implementation to preserve Valorian interests and citizen safety.
❦

Article V — Security, Defense and the Citizen Army

  1. The defense of Valoria is a shared responsibility. The Valorian Internal Service (VIS) maintains domestic order and supports civil defense; a trained citizen militia/army exists for territorial defense.
  2. Mobilization for external campaigns shall follow VRC and VRC procedures; where possible, Valoria emphasizes defensive readiness and avoiding entanglement in external power struggles.
  3. The VIS and Valorian forces coordinate with the UUR for joint defense obligations, subject to the VRC's guidance and citizen safeguards.
❦

Article VI — Culture, Identity and Local Autonomy

  1. Valorian culture, language, and heritage shall be preserved and promoted.
  2. Other cultures are respected within Valoria and in Alter Hafen's multicultural administration, provided they do not seek to undermine Valorian constitutional order.
  3. Acts intended to provoke unrest through cultural mockery are discouraged and may be addressed within the bounds of law and proportionality.
❦

Article VII — Treason, Loyalty and Public Office

  1. Officials and citizens sworn into office shall pledge loyalty to Valoria and respect Valorian obligations to the UUR.
  2. Treason, rebellion, or deliberate acts of subversion against Valoria or the UUR are prosecutable under Valorian law.
  3. The VRC has the constitutional authority to review alleged breaches by officials; adjudication remains the remit of the independent courts.
❦

Article VIII — The Rule of Law & Judicial Independence

  1. All citizens and officials are bound by this Constitution.
  2. Laws may be enacted by the VRC and require formal assent as defined in statute and VRC procedures.
  3. The judiciary shall remain independent and ensure due process, proportionality, and transparency.
❦

Article IX — Amendments and Citizen Referenda

  1. This Constitution may be amended by a two-thirds majority of the VRC and approval by the King, or by constitutional process initiated via citizen referendum when permitted by the VRC.
  2. For major structural changes (including alteration of VRC composition or core sovereignty clauses), a supermajority in the VRC and a citizen referendum are required.
  3. Procedures for citizen initiatives, thresholds, and referendum administration shall be detailed in implementing statute.
❦

Article X — Implementation

  1. This Constitution takes precedence over ordinary statute. Transitional provisions shall be enacted to reflect the VRC power distribution and the three-member VRC composition described herein.
❦

Article XI — The Territory of Determa and Colonial Administration

    Section 1 — Constitutional Status and Sovereignty

  1. Determa is officially recognized as a colonial territory under the sole sovereignty and protection of the Kingdom of Valoria, following formal approval and authorization from the United Union of Republics (UUR). The strategic military base left by the UUR in Determa shall be fully managed and controlled by the Valorian Crown to ensure regional security and defense.

    2. Section 2 — Local Administration and Governance

  2. The King of Valoria will manage the daily affairs, economy, and infrastructure of Determa himself, with support from the VRC. Valoria commits to establishing a stable, peaceful environment in Determa to prevent future revolutions, political unrest, or radical systemic changes. Valorian laws shall apply.

    3. Section 3 — Political Participation and Voting Rights

  3. The Valorian citizens of Determa shall have the right to vote in regular Valorian polls. As of May 28, 1926, Valoria will start the process of granting the people of Determa a "Valorianischer Pass". Only people with this citizenship will be allowed to vote in Valorian and Determian matters or actively engage in politics. People owning property in Determa shall have the right to vote in local polls specifically made for Determa. To ensure fair representation, the "Determianischer Rat" is established. This assembly can draft local policies and suggestions, subject to final approval by the Valorian Regional Council (VRC) and the King.

    4. Section 4 — Integration and Commerce

  4. Trade between Valoria and Determa shall be protected and promoted. Valorian enterprises shall have preferential trading rights within the territory to rebuild the local economy. Companies shall be encouraged to expand into Determa. The legal system of Determa shall be gradually aligned with the fundamental rights and rule of law of the Valorian Kingdom, ensuring safety, property rights, and fair trials for all inhabitants.

The Valorian Law Codex (VLC)

Established under the Constitution of the Kingdom of Valoria, ratified by the Valorian Regional Council (VRC) and confirmed by His Majesty the King and the Valorian Regional Council (VRC).

Title I — General Provisions

Article 1 — Purpose

The Valorian Law Codex ensures order, justice, and the protection of Valoria's sovereignty, culture, economy, and citizens.

Article 2 — Supremacy of the Constitution

All laws derive their authority from the Valorian Constitution. In conflict, constitutional principles prevail.

Article 3 — Equality Before the Law

All Valorians are equal before the law regardless of heritage, belief, or station. Nobility and office impose duties, not immunity.

Article 4 — Application of Justice

Justice shall be fair, transparent, and proportional. Punishment aims for rehabilitation, restitution, and deterrence rather than humiliation.

❦

Title II — Crimes Against the Realm

Chapter 1 — Treason and Rebellion

Article 5.1 — High Treason

Acts intending to overthrow, harm, or betray Valoria or the UUR are treason. → Punishment: 10–30 days imprisonment, exile, or loss of citizenship in grave cases.

Article 5.2 — Sedition and Civil Disorder

Organizing or leading demonstrations intended to violently oppose lawful authority or promote widespread disorder constitutes sedition. → Punishment: 3–15 days imprisonment or fine proportional to offense.

Article 5.3 — Foreign Collaboration

Deliberate cooperation with hostile powers to Valoria's detriment is a serious offense. → Punishment: 15–30 days imprisonment and asset measures appropriate to the breach.

Chapter 2 — Symbolic Acts and Loyalty

Article 6.1 — Insulting the Realm

Publicly mocking Valoria's institutions or symbols in a way that intentionally incites disorder may be sanctioned. → Punishment: fines or civic measures proportional to harm inflicted.

Article 6.2 — Royal Disrespect

Unlawful slander or targeted harassment of the Royal Family may be punished by administrative or civic remedies. → Punishment: fines, mandatory community service, or restorative measures.

Article 6.3 — Anti-Traditionalist Acts

Deliberate attempts to provoke unrest by promoting banned pre-reform symbols or identity-subversion may be addressed under this Codex. → Punishment: fines or brief civic corrective duties proportionate to the offense.

❦

Title III — Civil and Social Order

Chapter 1 — Conduct and Respect

Article 7.1 — Disrespect Toward Authority

Wilful refusal to comply with lawful civic orders or targeted harassment of officials is an offense. → Punishment: fines or civic service.

Article 7.2 — Threats and Harassment

Threatening or coercive behavior is prohibited. → Punishment: fines or short-term confinement as appropriate.

Article 7.3 — Destruction of Property

Intentional damage to public or private property requires restitution and civic remedy. → Punishment: restitution + fines or community work.

Chapter 2 — Theft, Fraud, and Deception

Article 8.1 — Theft

Taking property without consent is illegal. → Punishment: return of goods and restitution, or proportionate confinement.

Article 8.2 — Fraud

Deceit for material gain is prohibited. → Punishment: restitution + fines or confinement.

Article 8.3 — Exploitation of Loopholes

Exploiting statutory gaps to gain unfair advantage may be remedied by judicial penalty and legislative amendment where needed.

Chapter 3 — Public Morality and Health

Article 9.1 — Drug Prohibition

Non-authorized production, trade, or use of illegal narcotics is banned. → Punishment: custodial measures, confiscation, and rehabilitation where suitable. Licensed scientific/medical exceptions apply.

Article 9.2 — Disorderly Conduct

Public intoxication, violence, or indecent acts disturbing public order are punishable. → Punishment: fines or civic service.

❦

Title IV — Economic and Administrative Laws

Article 10 — Economic Loyalty

Valorian and allied enterprises are given preference in procurement when appropriate. Sabotage of domestic production or economic infrastructure is seriously penalized.

Article 11 — Corruption and Abuse of Office

Public officials engaging in bribery or abuse face dismissal, fines, and criminal sanction where warranted.

Article 12 — Tax and Contribution

Valoria imposes minimal regular taxation. In exceptional circumstances, mandatory civic contributions or service hours may be enacted under VRC law and VRC oversight.

❦

Title V — Rights and Safeguards

Article 13 — Right to Fair Trial

Every accused has the right to defense, representation, and an appeal process.

Article 14 — Privacy and Dignity

Private correspondence, homes, and personal property are protected from unlawful intrusion.

Article 15 — Emergency Suspension of Rights

In times of war or national emergency, certain rights may be temporarily limited by lawful decree, subject to prompt VRC review and judicial oversight.

❦

Title VI — Implementation and Interpretation

Article 16 — Judiciary Authority

The Royal Court of Valoria interprets this Codex. Its rulings set binding precedent.

Article 17 — Enforcement

The Valorian Internal Service (VIS) enforces this Codex under oversight of the Ministry of Justice and Security and subject to judicial review.

Article 18 — Amendments to the Codex

Laws may be modified by the VRC with VRC oversight and the assent required by the Constitution.

❦

Title VII — Special Provisions

Article 19 — Alter Hafen Autonomy

Alter Hafen retains municipal autonomy in local governance, trade administration, and cultural affairs consistent with Valorian law. Disputes between Alter Hafen officials and Valorian authorities are resolved through designated judicial or arbitration mechanisms.

Article 20 — Trade and External Agreements

Valoria may enter trade and alliance agreements (including free trade with the Astari). Such agreements shall be structured to preserve Valoria's neutrality posture where possible and to respect UUR obligations.

❦

Title VIII — The Royal Court and Judicial System

Article 21 — The Structure of the Court

Because Valoria is a practical kingdom with a focused population, the judicial system is kept simple and requires very few officials to run.

  1. The Royal Court: The highest court in the land. It consists of the King and the two VRC Lords, if the King wants it oor the public opinion gets the majority to agree that the VRC is needed (Only Valorians.).

    2. Article 22 — The Role of the King and Prevention of Rigging

    1. The King's Power: The King serves as the Chief Judge of the Royal Court. He opens hearings, listens to evidence, and ensures the law is followed.
    2. Anti-Rigging Rule: If the VRC Lords are present, a final verdict in the Royal Court requires a majority vote of the three-judge panel (The King and the two Law Speakers). Each holds an equal vote on guilt and punishment.
    3. Open Evidence Rule: All evidence and witness testimonies must be presented openly. No secret evidence may be used to convict a citizen.
    4. Public Record: Every court decision must be written down in the Public Ledger of Alter Hafen, making it impossible to change verdicts in secret.
    ❦

    Title IX — Marketplace, Trading, and Bidding Laws

    Chapter 1 — General Trade Rules

    Article 1 — The Open Market Rule

    All trades made in public markets must use official Valorian measures.

    Article 2 — The Verbal Bond

    A verbal agreement to trade goods is legally binding if witnessed by at least one neutral citizen or recorded in writing.

    Chapter 2 — Bidding and Auction Laws

    Article 9 — The Valid Bid

    Once a citizen places a bid in an official auction, they cannot withdraw it unless outbid by another party.

    Article 10 — Phantom Bidding Ban

    Sellers may not hire friends or use fake names to place false bids to drive up the price of their own items.

    Article 11 — The Hammer Rule

    An auction item belongs to the highest bidder the moment the auctioneer’s hammer falls or the official timer ends.

    Article 12 — Non-Payment Penalty

    If a winning bidder fails to pay for the item within 72 hours, the selling of the item can be cancelled, and the original winner must pay a fine equal to 30% of their bid. If the seller thinks he would deserve more than the 30%, they can sue the buyer and the Valorian court will decide.

    Article 13 — Minimum Reserves

    Sellers must state before the bidding starts if there is a minimum hidden price (reserve) the item must reach to be sold.

    Article 14 — Auctioneer Neutrality

    The person running the auction cannot bid on the items they are actively selling.

    ❦

    Title X — Civil, Property, and Daily Kingdom Laws

    Chapter 1 — Land and Housing

    Article 15 — Land Boundaries

    Boundary are made by the Crown or local Lords define property lines. Moving a boundary secretly is a major offense.

    Article 16 — Abandoned Property

    If a house or plot of land is left unused and unpaid for more than 60 days without notice, the Crown may reclaim it.

    Article 17 — Right of Way

    Citizens may cross private land if it is the only accessible route to a public road, well, or harbor, provided they cause no damage.

    Article 18 — Renting Fairness

    Landlords must give tenants at least 7 days' notice before eviction, except in cases of property destruction or non-payment.

    Article 19 — Collateral Protection

    If a citizen borrows money using their home or tools as collateral, the lender cannot seize the property without a Royal Court order.

    Article 20 — Building Safety

    Any structure built in a city must match the style.

    Chapter 2 — Contracts, Debts, and Labor

    Article 21 — The Written Word

    Partnerships or business agreements involving shared profits must be written down and signed by both parties.

    Article 22 — Debt Labor

    If a citizen cannot pay a legal debt, the Royal Court may order them to perform labor for the lender for a fixed number of days to clear the debt.

    Article 23 — Fair Wages

    Workers must be paid the amount agreed upon.

    Article 24 — Workplace Injury

    Workers have to look out for theyre safety themseselves, unless the employer guaranteed them safety.

    Article 25 — Money

    The VRC or King can request to see the salary.

    Article 26 — Contract Breaking

    Walking away from a half-finished building or service contract without a valid emergency allows the victim to claim damages in court.

    Chapter 3 — Harbor Rules

    Article 27 — Smuggling

    Bringing goods into Valoria without passing through official harbor checkpoints to avoid trade duties will result in the confiscation of the cargo.

    ❦

    Title XI — Public Order, Safety, and Military Laws

    Chapter 1 — Weaponry and Military Service

    Article 28 — Weapon Registration

    While people have the right to bear arms, firearms require a specific license from the VRC.

    Article 29 — Conscription Readiness

    In times of defensive emergency, every able-bodied citizen registered in the militia must report to their local post within 48 hours of the horn blast.

    Article 30 — Militia Care

    Citizens must keep their personal armor and weapons clean, functional, and ready for inspection by the VIS.

    Article 31 — Banned Public Drawing

    Drawing a weapon in an aggressive manner inside a tavern or public square, unless in clear self-defense, is a breach of peace.

    Article 32 — Desertion

    Running away from the militia line during a defensive battle is punishable by immediate exile and total loss of property.

    Chapter 2 — Public Peace and Neighborhood Rules

    Article 33 — Night Curfew Lights

    Chapter 3 — Public Goods and Infrastructure

    Article 34 — Road Maintenance

    Citizens are forbidden from digging up or blocking public roads without permission from the local Lord.

    Article 35 — Vandalism of Signs

    Defacing or destroying official Crown notices, bounty boards, or street signs will result in mandatory cleanup duties.

    Article 36 — Spying and Espionage

    Passing internal VRC documents or military defense plans to foreign states (even non-hostile ones) is treated as Foreign Collaboration.

    Article 37 — Diplomatic Immunity

    Official ambassadors sent by allies (such as the Astari) cannot be arrested by the VIS without the King’s personal approval, unless they pose an immediate physical threat.

    Article 38 — Bilateral Treaty Violations

    Any Valorian merchant who intentionally breaks a terms-of-trade agreement signed between Valoria and an allied nation can have their trading license revoked by the Court.

    ❦

    Closing Clause

    Let this Codex serve as a guardian of order, prosperity, and fair governance. Its aim is to preserve Valoria's role as a stable, trade-focused middle power — loyal to its treaties, protective of its people, and responsive to citizen influence.
